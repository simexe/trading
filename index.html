Simulation Instruction

  • Consider one single asset ABC that is traded in a financial market.
  • Participants will be split into 10 groups. 
  • Each group will act as a trader for the asset and will have to provide bid (buy) and ask (sell) prices.
  • Bid-Ask prices posted by each group will go to the central market authority to form transactions

Information

You are provided with partially known information about the asset fundamental value.

  • Public Information: all participating groups commonly know 75% of asset value.
  • Private Information: each group is informed by their analyst about the remaining 25% of possible asset value.

Set-up

The exercise runs over many rounds - as long as time allows today! You are asked to post bid-ask quotes based on your predictions on each round:

  1. At the end of each round, posted quotes will become visible to everyone.
  2. You will need to closely observe information made available at the end of each round to learn about price trends
  3. You may not know other groups' private information, however, you will be able to discover their private information via their posted prices
  4. Your quotes are expected to incorporate information publicized in previous rounds, while disclosing as little as possible (via quotes posted)
  5. At the end of the exercise, the group/individual with highest profit = margins x quantities bought/sold wins
  6. I’ll act as a limit order book clearing side and processing orders, matching the buy and sell sides

Objective

The goal is to learn about the tensions between bid-ask pricing of a security versus maintaining an active transaction flow.

Please be committed to your group members and actively engage.
