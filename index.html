Trading simulation is a practical exercise to simulate certain aspect of the real-world trading activities. Participants individually compete across 10-15 round and are expected to incorporate market information into their decision making and generate profit margins.

  • Consider one single asset ABC that is traded in a financial market.
  • Each group acts as an individual trader and will provide a bid (buy) and an ask (sell) prices at each trading round.
  • Bid-Ask prices posted by each group will go to the central market authority to form transactions

Information

You are provided with partially known information about the asset fundamental value.

  • Public Information: all participates commonly know 75% of asset value
    • 25%: Value increases to £525
    • 25%: Value stays the same at £500
    • 25%: Value decreases to £475
  • Private Information:
    • 25%: At the start of the year, each participant receives a private and noisy signal about the true asset value.

Objective

The goal is to learn about the underlying asset value and generate the highest profit by placing bid-ask prices
